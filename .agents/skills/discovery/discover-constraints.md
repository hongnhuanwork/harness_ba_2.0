# Purpose

To establish the boundary conditions and limitations within which the solution must be designed and built.

# When To Use

- Architectural design planning is active
- Compliance audits or regulatory checks are required
- Project kick-off meetings occur

# Inputs

- Company IT policies
- Regulatory compliance guidelines (GDPR, PCI, HIPAA, etc.)
- Project timeline and budget parameters

# Process

1. Extract budget, timeline, and resource limits from PMO documents.
2. Identify regulatory, security, and data privacy laws governing the system.
3. Document technical constraints (existing tech stack, required hosting, legacy database schemas).
4. Assess operational constraints (user literacy, training window, support team limits).
5. Compile into a Constraints Register and flag critical risks.

# Output

- Constraints Register (Technical, Legal, Financial, Operational)
- Critical Compliance Boundary Mapping

# Quality Checklist

- [ ] Regulatory requirements are linked to specific legal standards.
- [ ] Technical limitations are validated against current engineering stack.
- [ ] Constraints are clearly documented as immutable boundaries.

# Human Review Required

Yes. Lead Architect and Compliance Officer must review and sign off on constraints.
