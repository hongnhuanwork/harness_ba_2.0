# Purpose

To proactively map uncertainties and threats that could derail timelines, exceed budgets, or affect product quality.

# When To Use

- Project initialization
- Risk review meetings
- Major scope additions or architectural shifts

# Inputs

- Project Charter and Scope drafts
- Constraints Register
- Lessons learned from past projects

# Process

1. Review past project logs for recurring historical risks.
2. Brainstorm technical, operational, scope, and timeline risks.
3. Grade each risk on Likelihood (1-5) and Impact (1-5).
4. Calculate Risk Score (Likelihood x Impact) to prioritize items.
5. Propose high-level mitigation strategies for critical risks.
6. Log risks in the project risk register.

# Output

- Initial Risk Register
- Risk Heat Map

# Quality Checklist

- [ ] Every risk has a clear description, cause, and potential consequence.
- [ ] All risks with a score > 12 have a proposed mitigation plan.
- [ ] Risk grading is objective and consistent with PMO guidelines.

# Human Review Required

Yes. Project Manager or Scrum Master must approve the initial risk register.
